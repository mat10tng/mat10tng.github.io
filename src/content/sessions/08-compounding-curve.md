### take with you

```markdown
## Compounding checklist

If your reuse rate is stuck, check in this order:

1. Are notes available at all? (0% → 30%)
   → make previous notes loadable before each session

2. Are notes findable? (30% → 50%)
   → add types/categories, improve search

3. Is noise drowning signal? (50% → 65%)
   → compress raw notes into lessons, archive what's unused

4. Does the session have to know what to search for? (65% → 85%+)
   → proactively load relevant notes before the session starts
```

the system tracks its own reuse rate — what percentage of work sessions actually use something from a previous one. here's the real data, taken from the system's own self-assessments:

| date | reuse rate | what changed |
|---|---|---|
| march 20 | 0% | baseline. notes existed but nothing surfaced them |
| march 20 (session 5) | 38% | first sessions started loading previous notes |
| march 21 | 54% | added structured note types, better search |
| march 23 | 60% | weekly health check, compaction, search improvements |
| march 24 | 62% | daily triage auto-compacting noisy sessions into lessons |
| march 26 | 64% | monthly deep clean, duplicate detection |
| april 12 | 88% | proactive briefings — must-know notes loaded before every session |

the interesting part isn't the numbers — it's the plateaus. the system would hit a ceiling and stay there. adding more notes didn't help. writing more sessions didn't help. the ceiling only broke when I built a new mechanism.

### what broke each plateau

**0% → 38%** — just making previous notes available. before this, every session started from zero. the simplest change had the biggest impact.

**38% → 54%** — adding types to notes (session, fix, decision, lesson). untyped notes are hard to search. typed notes are findable by what they are, not just what they say.

**54% → 62%** — automated compaction. raw session notes pile up fast and become noise. compressing 30 session notes into 3 lessons makes the useful stuff findable again.

**64% → 88%** — proactive briefings. instead of waiting for the session to search for relevant notes, the system loads must-know notes automatically before work starts. the session doesn't have to know what to look for — the system tells it.

### the principle

measurement without mechanism is just observation. each jump on the curve came from building something — a structural change to how knowledge flows. "keep writing notes" doesn't compound. "make the system surface the right notes at the right time" does.
