## Principle
AI is inherently prone to over-extrapolate. To be a reliable consultant, a system must distinguish between what has been confirmed and what has been inferred. "Surface what and when, never how to solve."

## Confidence Modes
- **Precise Mode:** Maps only explicitly stated facts. Unresolved gaps are logged to a discovery backlog.
- **Autocomplete Mode:** Uses model priors to infer intermediate steps, marking them clearly for validation.
</div>

<div slot="technical">

## Implementation: Visual Proof
The system prompt enforces styling rules based on node confidence. Verified facts receive a solid stroke, while suggested paths are rendered as dashed lines. This preserves the human architect's role as the final authority on process truth.
</div>

<div slot="technical">
**Mermaid CSS classes:**
```mermaid
classDef verified stroke:#333,stroke-width:2px;
classDef suggested stroke:#999,stroke-dasharray: 5 5;

A[Verified Step]:::verified
B[Inferred Step]:::suggested
```
</div>
</Section>
