---
title: "the system that learns"
description: "88% of sessions now reuse something a previous session wrote. that wasn't always true."
pubDate: 2026-03-21
---

the knowledge base started as storage. dump notes in, search when needed. most notes were never found again.

the shift was measuring it. added a compounding score — what percentage of sessions actually reuse prior knowledge vs starting from scratch? first measurement: near zero. every session was reinventing.

so we started instrumenting. every search, every read, every capture gets logged. which documents surface in results? which ones actually get opened? high hits but zero reads = noise. low hits but high reads = gold.

then the KB started curating itself. sessions get captured automatically. after enough pile up, they get distilled into lessons. lessons that get cited enough get promoted. documents that never get cited get archived. the useful stuff floats up.

88% compounding score now. 9 out of 10 sessions pull from something a previous session wrote. the system remembers patterns I've forgotten, surfaces fixes I applied months ago, catches me before I repeat a mistake.

the metric made it real. without the compounding score, "the KB is useful" is just a feeling. with it, it's a number that either goes up or doesn't.
