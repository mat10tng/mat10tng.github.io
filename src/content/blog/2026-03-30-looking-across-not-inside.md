---
title: "looking across, not inside"
description: "bugs are visible inside a conversation. process problems are only visible across many."
pubDate: 2026-03-30
loop: "review"
---

inside a single conversation, everything looks fine. we find a bug, fix it, move on. maybe the fix took longer than it should have. maybe we went down the wrong path first. but it resolves, so it feels like it worked.

the pattern only becomes visible when you look across conversations. thirty of them. fifty. you start seeing shapes. a lot of conversations start the same way — dive into code, realize the approach was wrong, back up, start over. one time that's a misstep. forty times that's a process problem.

that's what review actually means here. not reviewing code. not reviewing the fix. reviewing how the work itself went, across enough data points that patterns emerge.

the first time I did this seriously, I pulled up a month of captured notes and just read through the outcomes. success, struggled, success, struggled, struggled, success. the struggled ones had something in common — no upfront discussion of approach. just straight to implementation.

that's not something you notice in the moment. in the moment you're focused on the bug. it takes stepping back and looking at the shape of many conversations to see it.

now I do this regularly. not on a schedule — just when something feels off. "a lot of recent work felt harder than it should." pull up the notes. look for the shape. usually there is one.
