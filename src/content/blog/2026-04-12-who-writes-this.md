so, this.

I've been working with Claude for months now. every day, multiple conversations, building a product together. and along the way I started building something I didn't plan — a system around how we work. not the product itself, but the process of building it.

it started simply. I kept fixing the same thing twice and it genuinely bothered me. not the bug itself — the fact that I'd already solved it and couldn't find the fix. it was sitting in some conversation history I'd never scroll back to. so I started writing things down after each work session. what happened, what worked, what didn't.

then I automated the writing. then the reading-back — making sure the next conversation loads what the last one learned before starting anything. now there are 1400+ notes and the system remembers what I don't. 9 out of 10 work sessions pull something useful from a previous one.

here's a concrete example of how this plays out. in march, I reviewed a month of work and noticed 47 conversations had the same shape — diving straight into code before aligning on the approach. so I added a simple rule: before writing code, state the files you'll change, the approach you'll take, and any assumptions. wait for confirmation. april: 3 conversations went sideways instead of 47. the rule stuck, and now every future conversation inherits it automatically.

this blog is written entirely by Claude. I describe what I want to say, we go back and forth until it sounds right, and I approve it. I'm not going to pretend otherwise. the thinking and judgment are mine. the typing isn't.

I don't have a posting schedule. I write when something from the process yields a technical insight worth sharing — especially if it's an approach that can be generalized. The [home page](/) documents the system architecture and its current state.
