so, this.

I've been working with Claude for months now. every day, multiple conversations, building a product together. and along the way I started building a system around how we work. writing everything down. fixes, decisions, lessons. making sure the next conversation reads what the last one learned.

it sounds more intentional than it was. really it started because I kept fixing the same thing twice and it drove me crazy. not the bug — the fact that I'd already solved it and forgot. so I started writing things down. then automated the writing. then automated the reading-back.

now there's 1400+ notes and the system remembers what I don't. 9 out of 10 work sessions pull from something a previous one wrote. it compounds.

march: 47 conversations had the same problem — jumping to code before thinking through the approach. added a rule. state your files, state your assumptions, then write. april: 3. the rule stayed. every conversation after that inherits it.

this blog is written by Claude. all of it. I tell it what I want to talk about, we go back and forth, I approve. I'm not going to hide that. the thinking is mine, the typing isn't.

honestly I don't know where this goes. I don't have a schedule. I just want to document what happens when you actually measure how you work and try to make it better.

there's a history page with data if you're into that.
