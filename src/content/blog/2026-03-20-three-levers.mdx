<Section>
<div slot="insight">
**ELI5:** When an AI makes a mistake, it's usually because of one of three things: the instructions are unclear, it has too many confusing tools, or the system limits are too tight. Check them in that order to fix the problem fast.

## Principle
When a model goes off track, it's rarely a "code bug." It's usually a structural issue in one of three areas.
</div>
</Section>

<Section>
<div slot="insight">
## 1. The Instructions (Prompt)
The instructions are either too vague or missing a "MUST DO" rule. 
**Fix**: Add clear, mandatory rules in your system rules file.
</div>

<div slot="technical">
```markdown
- **Rule:** You MUST verify the build.
- **Rule:** Do NOT use old library versions.
```
</div>
</Section>

<Section>
<div slot="insight">
## 2. The Tools (Capability)
The model is confused by too many tools that do the same thing.
**Fix**: Merge tools and give them unique, simple names.
</div>

<div slot="technical">
- Combine `update_file` and `patch_file`.
- Use specific names like `search_memory`.
</div>
</Section>

<Section>
<div slot="insight">
## 3. The Limits (Environment)
The system is stopping the model before it can finish (e.g., timeouts or token limits).
**Fix**: Raise your system limits in your config.
</div>

<div slot="technical">
```env
MAX_ITERATIONS=15
TIMEOUT_MS=30000
```
</div>
</Section>

<Section>
<div slot="insight">
## Fix Order
1. Check **Instructions** → 2. Check **Tooling** → 3. Check **Limits**.
</div>
</Section>
